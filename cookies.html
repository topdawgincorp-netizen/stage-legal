1. Scope: websites vs mobile apps

In plain English: This page is mostly about browser technologies. Our mobile apps use SDKs and device settings, not browser cookies.

For this Policy, “Trackers” means cookies, local storage, pixels, scripts, or similar technologies that run in a web browser when you visit websites we operate (for example, our marketing site or web app, if offered).

Native mobile applications (iOS / Android) do not use “cookies” in the browser sense. They may use software development kits (SDKs), device identifiers, and similar technologies as described in our Privacy Policy and our Subprocessors list. Read those documents for how we collect and use information in the app and through our APIs.

3. Why we use Trackers (web)

In plain English: We use web technologies to run the site, remember preferences, and—only if you enable them—measure usage.

Depending on the specific website and your choices, we may use Trackers for:

3.1 Strictly necessary / operational

These help with security, load balancing, session continuity, fraud prevention, or storing your cookie preferences. They are typically placed based on our legitimate interests in operating the Services, or contract necessity where you log in on the web.

3.2 Experience, analytics, and marketing (if used)

If we use optional analytics or marketing Trackers on a website, we will describe them here and, where required by law, obtain consent before non-essential Trackers run. Update this section with counsel to match your actual tools (e.g. product analytics on a web property).

Our static legal pages (for example, documents served from GitHub Pages for Terms and Privacy) may use minimal technical infrastructure from the host. Review your host’s documentation for any first- or third-party Trackers on that subdomain.

4. How long Trackers last

Session Trackers expire when you close the browser. Persistent Trackers remain for a period set by us or the vendor (see browser tools or our consent panel, if any). Align retention here with your internal schedules and Privacy Policy Section 8 (Retention).

5. How to manage preferences

In plain English: You can control many Trackers in the browser; some choices may be in our product.

  • Consent panel (if we offer one): Where we rely on consent for non-essential Trackers, you can withdraw consent at any time using the same interface.
  • Browser settings: You can view, block, or delete cookies and similar storage. See your browser’s help documentation.
  • Mobile devices: Advertising and tracking controls are often under system settings (e.g. Apple “Tracking,” Google Ads settings). See our Privacy Policy for rights and choices that apply to Personal Data.

If you block strictly necessary Trackers, parts of our web Services may not function correctly.
